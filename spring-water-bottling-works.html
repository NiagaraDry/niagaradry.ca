Bottles

The initial Spring Water Bottling Works bottles were embossed, no ACL or paper labels, and came in different glass colors.

"Niagara Dry"

As the company progressed, the Niagara Dry brand was introduced. The earliest Niagara Dry bottles will have Spring Water Bottling Works listed as the company instead of Niagara Dry Beverages like the later bottles. Once Spring Water Bottling Works changed their company name to Niagara Dry Beverages, it appears they also changed the company name on the labels of these early bottles. There is a picture on the Niagara Dry page under the "Labels" section showing an identical copy of the green label seen in the gallery below, but with Niagara Dry Beverages in place of Spring Water Bottling Works.

Other Flavors

Spring Water Bottling Works not only put out Niagara Dry Ginger Ale, but it seems they also put out other soda flavors such as Niagara Stone Ginger Beer and Niagara Lime Rickey. Both of those flavors would later become part of the Niagara Dry Beverages lineup, albeit with some minor name changes, to become Niagara Ginger Beer, Niagara Lemo-Lime Rickey, and Niagara Triple Soda.
